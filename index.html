Privacy Policy

Last updated: 29 March 2026

Herd ("we", "us", or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains what data we collect, how we use it, and your rights in relation to it. By using the Herd app, you agree to the practices described here.

Who we are

Herd is a daily opinion voting platform for UK users. We are operated as an independent product based in the United Kingdom. For privacy-related enquiries, contact us at: info@bherd.uk

What data we collect

When you create an account and use Herd, we collect the following information:

  • Account data: your email address and encrypted password, used to create and authenticate your account.
  • Demographic data: age range, gender, region, ethnicity, and religion. You provide this during onboarding. This data is used solely to generate aggregated demographic breakdowns of voting results.
  • Vote data: your yes/no responses to daily questions, linked to your demographic profile. Individual votes are never publicly attributed to you.

How we use your data

We use your data for the following purposes:

  • To operate your account and authenticate you securely.
  • To display how different demographic groups voted on each question.
  • To produce aggregated, anonymised demographic reports that may be shared with or sold to media organisations and researchers. These reports contain no personally identifiable information.
  • To improve the Herd product and user experience.

We do not use your data for advertising. We do not sell your personal data (name, email, or individual vote history) to any third party.

Demographic data and sensitive categories

Some of the demographic data we collect — specifically ethnicity and religion — falls into the category of "special category" data under UK GDPR. We collect this data only with your explicit consent, provided during onboarding. You may withdraw this consent at any time by deleting your account.

This data is only ever used in aggregate form. We never share individual-level demographic profiles.

Data sharing

We may share aggregated, anonymised voting data (e.g. "65% of 18-24 year olds in London voted Yes") with media clients and researchers. This data cannot be used to identify you.

We use Supabase (supabase.com) to store and manage your data securely. Supabase is our only third-party data processor. Your data is stored on servers within the European Economic Area.

Data retention

We retain your account and associated data for as long as your account is active. If you delete your account, all personally identifiable data — including your email address, demographic profile, and vote history — is permanently deleted within 30 days.

Your rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the following rights:

  • Right of access: you can request a copy of the data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification: you can ask us to correct inaccurate data.
  • Right to erasure: you can delete your account and all associated data at any time via Settings in the app.
  • Right to object: you can object to certain uses of your data.
  • Right to withdraw consent: where processing is based on consent, you can withdraw it at any time.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at info@bherd.uk

Cookies and tracking

Herd does not use cookies or any third-party tracking or analytics tools.

Children

Herd is intended for users aged 18 and over. We do not knowingly collect data from anyone under 18. If you believe a minor has created an account, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

Changes to this policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. We will notify you of significant changes via the app. Continued use of Herd after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

Contact

For any privacy-related questions or requests, please email: info@bherd.uk
